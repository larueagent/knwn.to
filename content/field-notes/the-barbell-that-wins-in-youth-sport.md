Codie Sanchez has a framework she keeps coming back to: the barbell wins. Maximum AI on one end. Maximum human on the other. Everything in the middle gets squeezed out — the generic apps, the automated drip sequences, the "solution" that's neither intelligent nor personal. The middle is where businesses go to die slowly.

She's talking about business models. But she's describing youth sport mental performance to the letter.

Here's what's getting squeezed right now: passive mental skills content. Generic confidence-building apps. Chatbot coaches that simulate empathy but don't know the athlete. Pre-recorded modules with a progress bar. These are middle-of-the-barbell products. They feel like support. They don't perform like it.

The athletes who will separate themselves over the next decade — the ones who show up to high-stakes moments with real mental skills, not borrowed mantras — will have two things.

Ruthless process on one end. A certified human in their corner on the other.

The process looks like this: an athlete.md that captures who they are as a competitor — their goals, their confidence patterns, their ACSI scores, their injury history, the mental state they were in before their last competition, what they're working on this week. An AI that reads that file before every session and shows up with context. No warm-up. No wasted time. Just signal.

The human looks like this: a CMPC — a Certified Mental Performance Consultant — who opens their first session with that athlete already knowing them. Not from a 20-minute intake form. From a structured, living record of how this athlete actually operates. The relationship starts at depth. The CMPC gets to do the work that only a human can do: build trust, read the room, make the call that no algorithm makes.

That's the insight people keep missing. The AI doesn't replace the sport psych. It makes the sport psych 10x more effective on day one.

Most platforms pick one side. They go full tech and lose the human. Or they put humans in the loop but drown them in intake friction and guesswork. Neither end of the barbell is actually loaded.

knwn.to is built on both ends. AI that's genuinely useful — athlete.md, First Read, LaRue — handling the organizational heavy lifting that used to eat practitioner time. And a team of certified sport psychologists and CMPCs on the other end, ready to do the work that actually moves the needle.

The middle is crowded. Both ends are where it's won.

That's what we're building.
